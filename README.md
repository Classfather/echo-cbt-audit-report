# ECHO Student Voices — CBT Centre Experience Audit
### Federal University of Technology, Akure (FUTA) | February 2026

---

## Overview

This repository contains the full audit report produced by **ECHO Student Voices Network**, a student-led accountability and advocacy initiative at FUTA.

The audit was designed to move student complaints about the Computer Resource Centre (CRC) from noise into structured, evidence-based data  and to present that data in a format that university administration could act on immediately.

It worked. A formal university memorandum was issued within **24 hours** of the initial findings being submitted and majorities of the recommendations were implemented.

---

## Key Findings

| Metric | Result |
|---|---|
| Total Responses | 317 students across 10 academic schools |
| Experienced Congestion | **83.9%** (266 of 317) |
| Waited More Than 1 Hour | **72.6%** (230 of 317) |
| Rated Crowding as High or Very High | **91.2%** (289 of 317) |
| Witnessed or Experienced a Health Incident | **63.4%** (201 of 317) |
| Physical Comfort Severely or Moderately Affected | **63.0%** (200 of 317) |
| Concentration Severely or Moderately Affected | **52.7%** (167 of 317) |

## About ECHO Student Voices

ECHO Student Voices Network is a youth-driven accountability platform founded at FUTA in July 2025. In under a year, ECHO mobilised over 1,000 students, secured a ₦200,000 grant from the Nigeria Youth Future Fund, and delivered data-driven audits that produced measurable institutional change.

ECHO exists on one principle: student problems should not stay as complaints. They should become evidence.
